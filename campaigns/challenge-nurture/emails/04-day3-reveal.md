# Email 4: Day 3 Recognition Protocol

## Subject Line Variants

### A: "Day 3 — the one that changes things" — recommended
Safe bet. Sequential framing with an escalation hook. "The one that changes things" creates weight without hype. Implies Day 3 is different from Days 1 and 2 — which it is. High open rate because curiosity is built on earned trust from two previous emails.

### B: "You are about to see something you cannot unsee"
Bold play. Permanent shift framing — once you see your operating system, you cannot go back to ignoring it. Dramatic but honest. Works because leaders who made it to Day 3 are invested enough to handle bold claims.

### C: "last day — and the most important one"
Personal touch. Lowercase, conversational, creates a finish-line feeling. "Most important one" validates their commitment — they saved the best for last. Appeals to completionists.

**Recommended A/B test:** A vs B
**Reason:** Tests whether understated confidence ("the one that changes things") outperforms dramatic declaration ("something you cannot unsee"). The winner shapes how we frame the assessment reveal in Email 5.
